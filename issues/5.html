🚨 Breaking

Your two congressmen just took opposite sides on a war

By the Wire Staff  ·  Sunday, Mar. 1

The U.S. and Israel struck Iran on Saturday. Multiple sources — including Iranian state television — are reporting that Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed in the attack. It’s one of the biggest military actions in years. And Westchester’s two House members, who both sit on the Foreign Affairs Committee, came out swinging in opposite directions.

Mike Lawler — that’s your rep if you’re in Rockland, Putnam, or the northern and eastern parts of the county — gave the operation his full backing. “The president was fully justified and legally justified,” Lawler said. “Any effort by my Democratic colleagues to say otherwise is a lie.” He says the White House notified Congressional leadership per the War Powers Act.

George Latimer — southern Westchester, into the Bronx — pushed back hard. “There was no structural briefing that I’m aware of that was in advance,” he told Westfair, drawing a comparison to the earlier Venezuela operation. His read: telling Republican leadership isn’t the same as consulting Congress. A full War Powers Act briefing is expected this week.

“The president was fully justified and legally justified.” — Rep. Mike Lawler (NY-17), Feb. 28

Two neighbors, two very different reads on whether we just started a war. Watch this space — the briefing this week will tell us a lot about where each of them goes next.

 
🏠 Community

After 51 years, he’s finally done

Jan Mitchell is retiring from Walter Panas High School in Cortlandt. He spent 51 years building the track and cross-country programs there — which means he started in 1975 and never really left.

Mitchell is a Hall of Famer. His athletes went to states. For a lot of Cortlandt families, he’s just always been there. He’s earned the rest.

 
🌙 Things to Know

Look up Monday night

A total lunar eclipse — a blood moon — will be visible right over Westchester in the early hours of Tuesday, March 3. Peak totality hits around midnight between Monday and Tuesday, and the forecast looks clear enough to see it. No special equipment needed. Just go outside, look up, and watch the moon turn red. It’s the kind of thing worth waking the kids for.
