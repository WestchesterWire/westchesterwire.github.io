🚨 Public Safety

Westchester is digging out — and nothing is running yet

Multiple sources  ·  Monday, Feb. 23

If you're reading this and wondering whether you should go anywhere today — no. The Blizzard of '26 delivered exactly what it promised: 12 to 18 inches across most of Westchester, with locally up to two feet in the northern part of the county. Wind gusts hit 55 mph overnight, visibility dropped to near zero, and the Blizzard Warning remains in effect until 6 p.m. tonight. This is the most significant winter storm to hit the region since January 2016.

Here's the timeline for when things come back online. The countywide road ban — no non-essential vehicles on any road in the county — lifts at noon. Bee-Line buses and Paratransit are suspended until 2 p.m., so don't plan on catching a bus before mid-afternoon. Metro-North is running an hourly reduced schedule on the Hudson, Harlem, and New Haven lines, with weekend-level service on the branch lines. The Hudson Rail Link bus and Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry shuttles are fully suspended. If you commute on the Port Jervis or Pascack Valley lines, those are halted entirely — check NJ Transit.

"We strongly urge everyone to stay home if possible." — County Executive Ken Jenkins

Schools are closed pretty much everywhere — Hawthorne-Cedar Knolls, The Masters School, the NY School for the Deaf, and Fordham's Westchester campus are all confirmed shut. Hastings-on-Hudson declared a village-wide snow emergency, canceling all activities. NYC called it a full snow day. If your district hasn't announced yet, assume the worst and check your school's website.

Power outages have been mercifully light so far — 161 Con Edison customers without power as of 8:15 a.m. out of 368,000+ — but with winds still gusting, that could change. Stay home. Dig out your car later. This one's still not done.

🚨 Public Safety

After Bronx mall chaos, county police are watching Cross County Center

On February 16, several hundred teenagers swarmed Bay Plaza Shopping Center in the Bronx, causing property damage and panic that ended with 18 arrests and multiple injuries. Within days, social media posts started circulating naming Westchester locations as potential next targets — including Cross County Center in Yonkers, along with other malls, libraries, and public venues across the county.

The county's Real Time Crime Center is actively monitoring social media for organizing activity, and Commissioner Terrance Raynor had a blunt message for teens: "Do not participate." Police in Yonkers, Mount Vernon, and New Rochelle are all on heightened alert. If you see a large group gathering at a public venue, the county wants to hear about it: 800-898-TIPS or tips@wccops.com.
