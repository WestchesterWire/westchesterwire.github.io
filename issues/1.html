🚨 Public Safety

Westchester is getting buried — and nobody should be driving right now

Multiple sources  ·  Sunday, Feb. 22

A historic nor'easter is dumping up to 20 inches of snow across Westchester County right now, with blizzard conditions — sustained winds topping 55 mph, near-zero visibility, and snowfall rates of 2-3 inches per hour — expected through Monday evening. This is the most significant winter storm to hit the region since January 2016.

Three separate states of emergency are in effect. Governor Hochul activated 100 National Guard members and placed 5,600 utility workers on standby statewide. County Executive Ken Jenkins declared a county emergency effective 6 p.m. Sunday, and Yonkers Mayor Mike Spano followed suit for the city. Snow Emergency Routes in Yonkers are active — your car will get ticketed or towed if it's parked on one.

"We strongly urge everyone to stay home if possible." — County Executive Ken Jenkins

Metro-North will run on a reduced hourly schedule Monday across the Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven lines. The Hudson Rail Link bus and Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry shuttles are suspended entirely. Widespread school closings are expected Monday — check your district's website tonight. Charge your devices, stock up on what you need, and check on your neighbors. This one's not messing around.

🏛 Politics & Government

The county just closed a $197 million budget hole — and you're paying for part of it

County Executive Ken Jenkins signed the 2026 county budget — a $2.5 billion spending plan that closes a $197.7 million gap through a mix of belt-tightening and your wallet. Nearly every county department got hit with 8% cuts, including Jenkins' own office. 180 positions have been eliminated through a hard hiring freeze (no layoffs). And for the first time in seven years, property taxes are going up — a 3.7% increase that works out to roughly $60 more per year on a $500K-assessed home.

Jenkins blamed the squeeze on federal policy shifts, calling it a "Trump tax increase" driven by slashed aid, tariff pressure, and ballooning mandated costs — healthcare alone jumped $65 million. On the capital side, the county's $605 million plan includes 94 new hybrid buses, $150 million for Yonkers wastewater upgrades, $50 million for housing programs, and $35 million for EV chargers at 77 sites across the county.
